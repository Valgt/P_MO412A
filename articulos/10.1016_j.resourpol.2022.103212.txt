                  Under the rapid economic development trend, exploring the resource optimization strategy of cultural and creative enterprises for sustainable socio-economic development is highly relevant. This study applies the recommendation system to decision-making and resource optimization of entrepreneurial projects for the current situation of complexs entrepreneurial enterprises in the cultural and creative industry (CCI). The neural network algorithm (NNA) is adopted to model project features, user's behavioral and content features. Finally, a recommendation and resource optimization model based on NNA is constructed for CCI-related entrepreneurial projects, and the model is evaluated and analyzed. The results demonstrate that with the increase in the training period, the model's recognition accuracy reaches 81.64%. Besides, the prediction error of the recommender system is minimized when the word vector length is 200, and the number of implied features is 200. Therefore, the entrepreneurial project recommendation and resource optimization model can significantly improve the recognition accuracy and reduce prediction errors, providing experimental references and contributing to the subsequent sustainable development of social economy and entrepreneurial resource optimization.

Under the rapid economic development trend, exploring the resource optimization strategy of cultural and creative enterprises for sustainable socio-economic development is highly relevant. This study applies the recommendation system to decision-making and resource optimization of entrepreneurial projects for the current situation of complexs entrepreneurial enterprises in the cultural and creative industry (CCI). The neural network algorithm (NNA) is adopted to model project features, user's behavioral and content features. Finally, a recommendation and resource optimization model based on NNA is constructed for CCI-related entrepreneurial projects, and the model is evaluated and analyzed. The results demonstrate that with the increase in the training period, the model's recognition accuracy reaches 81.64%. Besides, the prediction error of the recommender system is minimized when the word vector length is 200, and the number of implied features is 200. Therefore, the entrepreneurial project recommendation and resource optimization model can significantly improve the recognition accuracy and reduce prediction errors, providing experimental references and contributing to the subsequent sustainable development of social economy and entrepreneurial resource optimization.   Keywords Deep learning  AI Algorithms  Entrepreneurship resources  Sustainable development   Data availability No data was used for the research described in the article.   1 Introduction With the continuous development of economic globalization, college graduates are increasing yearly. As a result, the pressure of employment competition is constantly growing, making the social employment situation increasingly severe. Relevant data shows that the number of college graduates in China has increased from 5.754 million in 2010 to nearly 9 million in 2020, but the employment rate of college graduates was only about 70%. As China's economic development enters a new normal, the demand for independent innovation capability is constantly rising. The recommendation and resource optimization of many types of complex entrepreneurial projects, which promotes the economic restructuring and industrial upgrading, is an inevitable trend for sustainable economic development (Sharma et al., 2021a ; Bansal et al., 2022a ; Bouncken et al., 2021 ). Therefore, the Ministry of Education has promoted innovation and entrepreneurship education (IEE) reform in colleges and universities to meet the needs of sustainable economic development and improve the quality of entrepreneurship and employment of college graduates(Sharma et al., 2021a,b,c,d ). Meanwhile, resource optimization of cultural and creative enterprises has also become the focus of many scholars in related fields. In the interconnected era, various large-scale cultural and artistic events in China have increased in spurts. Meanwhile, comprehensive planning and construction of cultural performance venues have been carried out, fully reflecting the importance China attaches to the cultural and creative industry (CCI) and its determination to optimize the development of its resources in a comprehensive manner (Boubker et al., 2021 ). In the report of the National People's Congress, the goal of building a “strong socialist cultural state” in 2011 has been changed to “making the cultural industry a pillar industry of the national economy by 2020” as a clear goal for the sustainable economic development of CCI in the near future (Jena, 2020 ; Sharma et al., 2021b ). Against this background, it is urgent to research and analyze the current situation of college students' CCI-related entrepreneurship. College students are a particular group in the entrepreneurial army. They have a high education level and are receptive to new things. They become leading force in the CCI entrepreneurial teams. Moreover, the reform of IEE in Chinese colleges and universities has been comprehensively deepened. The success rate of college students' entrepreneurship reflects the level and quality of IEE to a certain extent and is related to the progress of the popularization of IEE (Hameed et al., 2021 ). Therefore, combining the two hot spots, CCI and college students’ entrepreneurship, is one of the trends in social entrepreneurship and employment development. Usually, the authenticity and quality screening of entrepreneurial projects is highly complicated and challenging (Sharma & Kraus et al., 2022 ). Therefore, choosing appropriate cultural and creative projects is imperative to increase the entrepreneurship rate. The recommendation system (RS) is critical for information retrieval and filtering. It can be applied to project decision-making and resource optimization in cultural and creative industries by actively searching and analyzing users' historical behavioral information to achieve accurate recommendations of user preferences. This system performs well in intelligence, accuracy, and personalization (Cunningham et al., 2021 ). With the continuous improvement of science and technology, artificial intelligence (AI) technology is extensively applied in various industries. Neural networks can extract the characteristics of many entrepreneurial projects and resources to achieve automatic optimization of entrepreneurial resources (Nica and Stehel, 2021 ). Moreover, the RS via the neural network algorithm (NNA) can continuously modify the recommendation for entrepreneurial projects based on the user's real-time behavior. Precisely, when the interest drifts of users, it can recommend diverse information for the user (Chalmers et al., 2022 ). When college students start a business, they can choose the appropriate entrepreneurial mechanism and decision-making model according to the RS, significantly improving the entrepreneurial effect. In summary, exploring the optimization of projects and resources in CCI has the theoretical significance of promoting the optimization of economic structure and sustainable development and the practical importance of promoting the increase of entrepreneurship rate of college students. This study innovatively adopts RS for decision-making and resource optimization of CCI-related entrepreneurial projects in response to many different types of entrepreneurial projects. Besides, NNA is employed to model the project characteristics, user's behavior, and content features. Finally, a recommendation and resource optimization model of CCI-related entrepreneurial projects is constructed based on the NNA. This study provides an experimental basis for intelligent and sustainable development of the economy. The structure of this study is as follows. Section 1  describes the research background, motivation, purpose, innovation, and contribution. Section 2  is a literature review, summarizing the strengths and weaknesses of the research of scholars in related fields, highlighting the value of this study. Section 3  constructs a model based on the NNA for recommendation and resource optimization of entrepreneurial projects based on the NNA because there are various types of CCI-related entrepreneurial projects. In addition, the performance of the model is evaluated experimentally. Section 4  compares and analyzes the model constructed here with the models proposed by scholars in related fields. Section 5  briefly describes the experimental results, explains the limitations of this study, and presents the subsequent outlook.  2 Related work 2.1 Optimization and development status of CCI-related entrepreneurial resources Today, the public constantly mentions the slogan of mass entrepreneurship and innovation, and CCI has become the direction of choice for many entrepreneurs. Many scholars have researched resource optimization in entrepreneurship-related fields. Wu and Lin (2021)  combined entropy weight and grey relational analysis to form an evaluation index system considering ambiguity and complexity. The results indicated that cities in the eastern region and island cities have a higher capacity to invest in the utilization of cultural resources than cities in the western region. Meanwhile, the economic benefits of culture are not limited to certain CCIs but extend to the economy as a whole. The regional economy has been effectively revitalized through stimulus policies to build communities. The authors effectively improved the resource generation capacity of the city. Lazzaro and Noonan (2021)  evaluated a range of benefits and barriers to the increasing reliance on crowdfunding of the arts and CCI and proposed a simple financing model for startups. They also critically analyzed and compared the regulatory frameworks in the United States and the European Union. Analysis results indicated that these regulatory frameworks might improve the effectiveness of the creative industries. Finally, the authors put forward some policy recommendations and future research directions. Liu et al. (2021)  gave a detailed analysis and optimization of a modern AI teaching information system based on determining the network architecture and optimizing the overall structure of the network through requirements analysis. Meanwhile, they proposed a multi-class interaction optimization algorithm in combination with the clustering method based on Euclidean distance to enhance the local search capability of the algorithm. It was found that the optimization system has high solution accuracy and excellent stability and performs well in engineering optimization problems, thus verifying the effectiveness of the strategy. Wang et al. (2022)  analyzed the relationship between venture capital, firm performance, and supply chain integration. They also explored the positive impact of supply chain technology optimization innovation and venture capital on the performance improvement of CCI-related enterprises. The results found that optimizing the supply chain structure and production activities could improve the performance of suppliers and venture capital firms.  2.2 Analysis of the application status of Deep Learning in the RS Today, Deep Learning (DL) algorithms are extensively used in all walks of life. They have achieved remarkable results, especially in personalized RSs. Because of the advantages in prediction accuracy, DL has attracted the attention of scholars in related fields. Jin et al. (2020)  proposed a human-in-the-loop parallel learning framework and its application in an end-to-end recommender system that imitates and enhances the behavior of professional signal control engineers. Experimental evaluations showed that the signal RS significantly improved traffic efficiency. Lv et al. (2020)  constructed a cognitive computing model based on context-aware data flow by optimizing the decision tree algorithm in machine learning. They found that applying the model algorithm can ensure the accuracy and stability of behavior classification. It is of great significance for operators to analyze user behavior and develop personalized services. Wen (2021)  designed an intelligent background music system based on DL and the Internet of Things technology and applied it to smart homes. Finally, the experimental results showed that the feature extraction algorithm based on the middle layer feature structure has the highest recognition rate for indoor scenes, about 87.6%. In contrast, the recognition rate of the Gabor feature algorithm for scene classification and recognition is generally about 20%. Gupta et al. (2021)  reviewed and surveyed relevant research in the field of decision support systems and identified gaps and future research opportunities in AI, such as DL, for prediction and learning, decision-making, and optimization. Sharma & Erkut et al. (2020)  analyzed the effectiveness of AI in stock market forecasting by a systematic evaluation. Ultimately, the analysis revealed that AI has extremely significant advantages in stock market prediction and recommendation that can pave the way for future research in the field of finance.  2.3 Status of sustainable economic development With the rapid development of the global economy, a sustainable economy has become the driving force for the continuous development of the era. Sharma et al. (2021d)  used wavelet coherence and the phase difference mechanism to understand the correlation of relevant indicators in the financial domain before and during the coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19). The results found a short-term unidirectional causality from the sustainability index to the conventional index and a bidirectional causality in the medium and long term. This indicates a leading long-term consistency with the sustainability index during COVID-19. Bansal et al. (2022b)  proposed a thematic framework to outline the synergy between circular economy principles and business sustainability from the perspective of business sustainability. At the same time, they effectively measured the practical applicability of the combination of circular economy principles and business strategies and models. Sharma et al. (2021c)  introduced the regulatory effects of renewable energy use and human capital to understand whether they can offset agricultural emissions in this Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation economies. The results showed that human capital is not enough to offset the impact of pesticide use on greenhouse gas emissions. Besides, the interaction between renewable energy and pesticide use suggests that adopting renewable energy in the agricultural sector can reduce the impact of pesticide use on greenhouse gas emissions and support the realization of a few sustainable development goals.  2.4 Summary The above studies suggest that intelligent RS has been broadly used in various fields. However, studies on resource optimization and economic sustainability of CCI combined with smart RSs are relatively rare. Therefore, this study introduces DL algorithm to build an intelligent RS related to entrepreneurial project decision-making and resource optimization to improve the success rate of entrepreneurship. There are still problems with the rating matrix's sparsity and users' cold start. Integrating user characteristics into the RS and reducing the impact of the sparsity of the rating matrix on the recommendation results is essential. The above algorithm is further extended. A DNN replaces the matrix factorization algorithm to model user behavior and content characteristics. The user's feature information is also integrated into the RS to resolve the user's cold start problem and improve the accuracy of the RS. Fig. 5   reveals the recommendation and resource optimization model of CCI-related entrepreneurial projects based on NNA. The network in Fig. 5  contains two neural network structures: the user network model on the left and the item network model on the right. The network structure of the item's latent feature extraction uses Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to process unstructured text information and uses word embedding and one-hot encoding to process other features. The user model mainly includes two parts: the user's historical behavior feature modeling and the user's content feature modeling. The Restricted Boltzmann Machine (RBM) algorithm is used to process the user's historical behavior information as the network's input. The content features are processed in a similar way. Specifically, CNN is used to process text features, and word embedding and one-hot encoding are used for other features. Therefore, the processing procedures of the model include feature processing and splicing, layer-by-layer abstraction, and scoring output in the fully connected layer. First, user features and item features are processed separately. After each feature is processed, user features and item features are spliced respectively to generate high-dimensional space vector representations of users and items. Second, the fully connected layer abstracts the high-dimensional space vectors representing users and items layer by layer. Then, several fully connected layers extract features from users and items layer by layer and finally output the hidden features of the item and the user, which are in the same vector space. Third, using the idea of matrix decomposition to calculate the inner product of the IFs of users and items output by the two networks can obtain the output of the network, that is, the user score. It is necessary to reduce the dimension of the user's rating data before inputting it into the neural network to further reduce the impact of data sparsity on the model. The user's rating vector is dimensionally reduced by using the RBM algorithm. The visible layer reflects the user's rating data, and the hidden layer is used to represent the dimensionality-reduced data. Taking the dimensionality-reduced rating data as the input of the DNN to reconstruct the user's network structure. Of course, the input of the user network includes other feature information of the user, such as the region, profile, areas of concern, and purpose. Finally, the model is trained and generates the recommendation result. Fig. 6   provides the specific process. As shown in Fig. 6 , model training is divided into unsupervised pre-training and supervised training. Pre-training mainly reduces the impact of data sparsity on model training by training the RBM network. Meanwhile, relatively dense features are extracted from the sparse scoring data. In the supervised training stage, the user rating features obtained by pre-training are combined with other content features of the user as the input of the user network. The item's text and other features are used as the input of the item network. Then, the neural network maps user and item features into the same high-dimensional space. It is found that there are no publicly available datasets on the Internet that can be used for the entrepreneurial project RS by browsing and comparing relevant datasets on the Internet. Therefore, this study uses web crawlers to collect information from the entrepreneurial project information platform. The processing methods for different information are as follows. First, missing data is processed. The data of startup projects with too few users, missing data, or too many users were deleted. There are 7759 entrepreneurial projects and 7763 users in total. A total of 248,517 user rating data are obtained through the processing method of implicit feedback data, and the sparsity of the data is 99.59%. Second, entrepreneurial project data is processed. The entrepreneurial project characteristics include the project number, name, field, financing round, project label, and status. Only a few entrepreneurial projects have missing values, and these entrepreneurial projects with missing values are directly deleted. All other entrepreneurial projects are reserved. Third, user data is processed. The user characteristics include the user's region, profile, areas of concern, and purpose. The processing methods of these data are as follows. The regional data, such as Beijing and Shanghai, are processed by one-hot encoding. Then, user profiles, such as serial entrepreneurs from 2010 to 2016, are unstructured text data. The same processing method is adopted as the description text data of the project. In other words, the text CNN is used to process the user's profile after word segmentation. Third, users can focus on multiple fields, such as finance, the Internet, and education, up to a maximum of 21. Since the total number of fields is small, it can be processed by one-hot encoding, and each user's concerned field is encoded as a 21-dimensional vector. Fourth, the user's purpose involves understanding new companies and new products and looking for investment objects. There are a total of 9 users' purposes. Each user may have multiple purposes. One-hot encoding is used for processing, and each user's goal is encoded as a 9-dimensional vector. Fifth, scoring data is processed. The scoring data is calculated and obtained from the user's historical behavior data and processed by the implicit feedback data processing method (Li et al., 2022 ). Each row of the processed score data represents a user's score data. The score data includes three columns: the user ID, the entrepreneurial project ID, and the score. The hyperparameters are set as follows for the CNN framework to achieve the desired prediction results. The number of training epochs is 65; the learning rate is 0.002; the batch size is 128; the convolution kernel size is 1*3; the AF is ReLU; the Dropout Rate size in the CNN framework is 0.5; the optimizer is Adam. Comparative analysis is conducted on the representative advanced CNNs, including AlexNet (Jamshidi et al., 2020 ), DenseNet (Kalinov et al., 2020 ), VGGNet (Zhou et al., 2020 ), IGCNet (Javed et al., 2021 ), the model proposed by Liang et al. (2021) , and the model reported here. Algorithm performance is analyzed from Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1 value, and Root Mean Square Error (RMSE). The experimental environment is as follows. The operating system is Ubuntu 16.04 64-bit; the Central Processing Unit is Intel(R) Xeon(R) CPU E 5 -2682 v4@ 2. 50 GHz; memory is 16G; hard disk is 500G SSD; development language is Python; the DL framework is Keras (backend uses TensorFlow).   4 Results and discussion 4.1 Analysis of recognition accuracy of entrepreneurial project recommendations under different algorithms The model constructed here is compared with advanced CNNs and the proposed model by Liang et al. Third, when the number of entrepreneurial projects is 300, the recognition accuracy of the model for project resource optimization reaches 81.75%. These findings can contribute to the sustainable development of the social economy and the optimization of entrepreneurial resources. Still, this study has some shortcomings. First, the algorithm experiment is extended to the distributed platform. Because the data set of the entrepreneurial platform information used here is relatively small, relevant experiments can be completed on a single machine. However, when the amount of data continues to increase, the single-machine operation may lead to the problem of slow operation. It is essential to conduct a comprehensive analysis of the running speed of the model in the future. Moreover, this study only recommends and makes decisions on entrepreneurial projects in the information of the entrepreneurial project platform. Follow-up research should pay attention to cross-domain recommendation issues, such as news data and other information in the entrepreneurial project platform. The cross-domain information also reflects users’ interests to a certain extent. Therefore, how to add cross-domain information to the recommendation algorithm will be an essential research direction for the improvement of the subsequent RS.
