                  Solid waste collection (SWC) has been one of the growing challenges throughout the world due to the development of urbanization and uncontrolled growth of the population. It is a complex process that is expensive and requires the elaborate logistics management and in-depth mathematical computation. Hence, there is an urgent necessity to develop an efficient waste collection process while saving time, fuel, labour, maintenance costs, and carbon emissions. This goal of this is paper is to deliver a comprehensive review of the SWC system concerning solid waste collection objectives, constraints, the different optimization approaches, and models, along with their strengths and weakness. The different types of optimization objectives and constraints on SWC are highlighted towards improving solid waste collection and minimizing the cost. Furthermore, this review has classified the available optimization methods into different categories and technologies to provide a clear understanding for the readers. Besides, future research gaps are identified through the investigation of various key issues and challenges of the existing methods. Moreover, the significant contributions of SWC in connection to sustainable development goals (SDGs) are explained thoroughly. Finally, the review delivers some important proposals that would be beneficial to the researchers and policymakers to design an efficient and robust SWC system toward achieving SDGs.

Table 5     Approaches Sub-groups Advantages Disadvantages   Modeling approach Deterministic • It has the necessary theoretical basis to evaluate various factors.  • Avoids choosing variables or performance randomly.    • Applies the subjective measures to assess the performance.     Stochastic • Provides enhanced convergence to the global minimum.  • It has a fast convergence rate.    • High variance results in heavy fluctuation in the objective function     Fuzzy • Flexible and customizable.  • Uses simple mathematics to address non-linear and complex system.  • It has a rapid operation and high precision.    • Needs human expertise to develop fuzzy rules.  • Requires longer run-time of system.     Probabilistic • It has a firm theoretical basis.  • The results are ranked by the decreasing order of probabilistic.    • The method needs initial guessing.  • It assumes independent index terms.     Solution approach Conventional • Shows good performance in the case of small-scale models.    • Conventional methods are not very suitable in case of different uncertainties.  • Unsatisfactory solutions for non-convex and/or large-scale models.     Heuristic • Obtains the near-optimal solutions in a fraction of the time required by optimal methods.    • Lack of precision and needs a long execution time.  • Do not guarantee to find optimal solutions.     Meta-heuristic • Flexible in terms of incorporating constraints, objectives, different conditions, and complicating factors.  • Achieves near-optimal solutions with less computational resources.    • A larger number of executions with different parameter settings are required, thus leading to sensitive solutions. Prof. Jiri Jaromir Klemeš Solid waste collection (SWC) has been one of the growing challenges throughout the world due to the development of urbanization and uncontrolled growth of the population. It is a complex process that is expensive and requires the elaborate logistics management and in-depth mathematical computation. Hence, there is an urgent necessity to develop an efficient waste collection process while saving time, fuel, labour, maintenance costs, and carbon emissions. This goal of this is paper is to deliver a comprehensive review of the SWC system concerning solid waste collection objectives, constraints, the different optimization approaches, and models, along with their strengths and weakness. The different types of optimization objectives and constraints on SWC are highlighted towards improving solid waste collection and minimizing the cost. Furthermore, this review has classified the available optimization methods into different categories and technologies to provide a clear understanding for the readers. Besides, future research gaps are identified through the investigation of various key issues and challenges of the existing methods. Moreover, the significant contributions of SWC in connection to sustainable development goals (SDGs) are explained thoroughly. Finally, the review delivers some important proposals that would be beneficial to the researchers and policymakers to design an efficient and robust SWC system toward achieving SDGs.   Keywords Solid waste collection  Optimization techniques  Optimization objectives  Optimization constraints  Sustainable development goals  Solid waste collection technologies    1 Introduction Urbanization is rapidly increasing in developing countries. The increasing trend towards urbanization and population growth combined with the growing concern about the negative environmental impacts have created a critical situation for the management of household solid waste (Chen, 2018 ). In line with that, ineffective waste management and weak policies may lead to the degradation of valuable land resources, a rise in the land price and the creation of long-term environmental and human health problems (Breukelman et al., 2019 ). Hence, a sustainable solid waste collection (SWC) model is essential taking into consideration public health principles, legislative, social, economic and other environmental impacts (Ramos et al., 2018 ). The SWC model includes many functional elements such as collection, sorting, separation, transfer and disposal to address aesthetic, health, economic, energy conservation and other environmental attentions (Hoque and Rahman, 2020 ). Among all the steps, the collection of waste is regarded as the most significant essentials toward achieving sustainable SWC. Nevertheless, SWC is a complex service that requires the consideration of different operational and logistical activities, equipment maintenance, preparation crews, health, safety, large expenditures and environmental issues, awareness of consumers and destination facilities for different types of waste (Das et al., 2019 ). Therefore, the design of an intelligent SWC is increasingly explored to enhance waste collection efficiency. The research questions of this review can be written as follows;i. What are the objectives and parameters constraints required to design an optimized SWC toward reducing the cost, duration and emission?  ii. What are mathematical modeling approaches, intelligent systems and advanced technologies reported in the literature to achieve the objectives of SWC?  iii. What are the key issues that need to be taken into account while designing the SWC model?  iv. What are the impacts of SWC on sustainable development goals (SDGs)?    A great deal of research works has been published on finding the appropriate objectives in SWC. The most common optimization objectives include waste collection route optimization by reducing the route length (Sarmah et al., 2019 ), time optimization (Carlos et al., 2019 ), waste content (Abdel-Shafy and Mansour, 2018 ), cost optimization (Rathore and Sarmah, 2019 ) and environmental impacts (Ali et al., 2018 ). To optimize these parameters in a more realistic environment, the various constraints have been considered. Since waste is collected by a waste collecting vehicle, the most common constraint in different studies is the vehicle capacity constraints (Dotoli and Epicoco, 2017 ), time constraints (Bányai et al., 2019 ), characteristic of collection component constraints (Yadav and Karmakar, 2019 ), environmental constraints (Pujara et al., 2019 ), regulatory constraints (Roberts et al., 2018 ), etc. Globally, the research on solid waste model development is advancing tremendously. A lot of SWC models have been reported in literature aiming to have a lower operating cost, labour hours, collection time, travel distances, and emissions (Pires et al., 2019 ). Several methods are being applied to establish a SWC model such as mixed-integer linear programming based exact method, deterministic approach, stochastic approach, probabilistic approach, fuzzy approach, etc. Apart from that, numerous meta-heuristic approaches have been gaining popularity in waste collection optimization due to their simplicity, flexibility and robustness (Akhtar et al., 2017 ). Recently, the development of the intelligent technologies, such as geographical information system (GIS), a global positioning system (GPS) and remote sensing (RS), radio-frequency identification (RFID), ZigBee, image processing and intelligent bins combined with different sensors, have been introduced and applied to develop an effective SWC system (Hannan et al., 2015 ). The advanced technologies not only reduce the maintenance cost and travel distance but also save a significant amount of time and carbon emissions (Abdallah et al., 2019 ). The investigation on various crucial issues related to SWC model needs to be taken into consideration. SWC operation can vary among countries due to various socio-economic factors including population size, financial status, road congestions, air pollution, human labour availability, amount of collection vehicle, etc. The waste collection process could also differ due to type building such as residential and commercial. Besides, the development of multi-objective functions and the feasibility of various technologies are considered as main barriers to execute the SWC model operations. The safety and waste disposal are reported as some other key issues of SWC. The implementation of an optimal SWC system can contribute to multiple SDGs and their targets related to improving the sustainable smart city/smart village through environmental protection, public health protection and poverty reduction. These targets can be achieved by efficient SWC through responsible consumption and production, execution of the renewable energy share in the global energy mix, prevention of marine pollution, mitigation of greenhouse gases (GHG) emissions in large quantities, reduction of chemical and biological hazardous wastes, development of sustainable cities, creation of employment opportunity toward economic growth and poverty reduction. The main contributions of this study are to deliver an in-depth discussion of SWC optimization objectives, constraints, methods and their challenges towards targeting SWC. Firstly, this review investigates the different waste collection objectives, constraints and their relation with sustainability. Then, the classification of optimization techniques is provided according to methods, technologies, benefits, and drawbacks. After, the challenges and issues of different optimization approaches are highlighted. Next, this work establishes the relationship of SWC with SDGs highlighting the influences of efficient collection process and technology towards future sustainability with regard to good health, clean water and sanitation, poverty reduction, sustainable cities, renewable energy production, responsible consumption, and production, economic growth, and emissions savings. Finally, this review delivers some effective suggestions for future system development. The remainder of the paper is organized into six sections. Sections 2  covers the review screening method. The SWC optimization and constraints are described under section 3 . The SWC methods, optimization techniques, technologies are explained in section 4 . The discussion, key issues and challenges of SWC are highlighted in section 5 . The connection between and SWC is established in section 6 . Section 7  concludes the review with some proposals.  2 Surveying methodology The survey process was carried out according to content analysis to fulfill the target of this review. The Scopus databases were used to explore the literature for this review. The Google Scholar, Web of Science, and Scopus were chosen for the citation. The authors employed several keywords to search for relevant publications within the scope of the research including SWC, optimization methods, optimization objectives, optimization constraints; SWC modeling approaches,  and SDGs . Although numerous articles were found from the search, suitable literatures were selected by evaluating the title, keywords, abstract, paper content and the main topic of interest of the journal. Subsequently, the relevant articles were chosen based on the impact factor, review process and citation. The results of this screening method were organized into five groups. Firstly, the different SWC objectives, along with their constraints were comprehensively reviewed. Secondly, the SWC modeling approaches, advanced technologies, strengths, weaknesses and contributions were discussed. Thirdly, the review discussed various issues and challenges of different optimization approaches. Fourthly, the review analyzed the relationship of SWC with SDGs focusing on health, sanitation, poverty, economic progress, smart cities, renewable energy and emissions. However, it is not that easy to find an appropriate range of constraints in the optimization collection process. There are always numerous issues and challenges in every region. The SWC model becomes more practical by taking into account more constraints. It may include the lobbying aspect in the selection of an assigned company responsible for the task. Sometimes the optimization has to be performed considering the preset regulations set by an authority. The progress of deterministic, stochastic, fuzzy, probabilistic, conventional and heuristic methods is highlighted to explain the SWC operation. 5.1 Modeling approaches of SWC The modeling approaches of SWC is used in common to minimize the waste collection distance, cost, time, or any other objectives, but these techniques suffer from poor computational efforts and work better in more simple situations. 5.1.1 Deterministic and stochastic approaches In the study of solid waste optimization, an important concern is whether the model is deterministic or stochastic (Asefi et al., 2019b ). A large part of the literature on SWC has been reported using the deterministic formulations, where all problem variables are known at the initial point of model development. However, this assumption may not hold true in real-world cases. Uncertainty can influence parameters such as the amount of waste generated, transportation costs, waste processing and waste transformation coefficients. The way forward could be to apply the stochastic models that can be designed with the assumptions of the probability distributions using random data elements or the past data. The authors in (Chen et al., 2016 ) considered multiple sources of uncertainties for waste management planning in the city of Xiamen, China which integrates inventory model, inexact chance-constrained programming, mixed-integer linear programming and interval-valued fuzzy programming. In (Gambella et al., 2019 ), A two-stage multi-period stochastic programming is introduced with realistic constraints for waste flow allocation aiming to reduce the total management costs. A work in (Chen et al., 2017 ) developed an approach based on stochastic fractional inventory-theory for SWC integrating inventory model, stochastic linear fractional programming and mixed-integer linear programming under several constraint-violation risks. In (Di Corato and Montinari, 2014 ), stochastic dynamic programming is applied to design optimal SWC programs under the uncertainty of recyclables cost. Another work in (Sun et al., 2013 ) considered uncertainties such as waste generation rates and waste parameters to develop a joint chance-constrained programming model.  5.1.2 Fuzzy and probabilistic approaches Many researchers have examined various uncertainties to develop an efficient and realistic SWC model. The probabilistic approach, robust approach, fuzzy approach are some methods that can model a SWC system with uncertainties, as presented in Table 2  . Xu et al. (2016)  suggested an enhanced fuzzy robust optimization model which can tackle fuzzy constraints with multiple algorithms and can integrate fuzzy violation variables under complex uncertainties. Bui et al. (2020)  applied the fuzzy Delphi method to identify the major barriers of waste management practice including technical, economical, human resource and information sharing. Jahanfar et al. (2017)  introduced a novel probabilistic approach under uncertainties including the density, friction angle and cohesion of solid waste slopes. Xu et al. (2014)  developed an interval joint-probabilistic mixed-integer programming (IJMP) method by assigning joint probabilistic constraints such as random variables, interval values uncertainties and violation levels in waste management practice constraints.   5.2 Solution approaches of SWC This section discusses the different solution approaches of SWC including conventional, heuristic and meta-heuristic methods. 5.2.1 Conventional approaches The conventional approaches cannot deliver satisfactory solutions while optimizing multiple objectives and handling uncertain parameters. However, the conventional approaches can transform multiple objectives into a weighted sum single objective which is less complex to solve. The research is advancing to enhance the mathematical programming which can accept uncertain input parameters and cope with many real-world problems. The mixed-integer linear programming (MILP) approaches are receiving attention for modeling a problem with variables changing within real numbers or the integers. However, MILP has shortcomings in terms of large computational efforts and risk of high-dimensionality. In (Yousefloo and Babazadeh, 2020 ), the MILP model is designed for SWC to optimize the total costs taking into account the economic and environmental aspects. In (Mavrotas et al., 2015 ), the authors applied the MILP model and the generation of Pareto optimal solutions to optimize the net present value (NPV) and the total GHG emissions for SWC in the Athens region in Greece. In (Erdinç et al., 2019 ), MILP based optimal SWC is established to identify the best route for waste collection in Istanbul, Turkey. The cluster-first-route-second heuristic method was used to solve the capacitated arc routing problem (CARP) by the Vertex-one Centre algorithm (Otoo et al., 2014 ). In this method, at first, the groups are formed by clustering the customers and then allocated to waste vehicles (phase I); after which, efficient routes are identified for each group (phase II). The authors in (Hemmelmayr et al., 2013 ) applied the heuristic of the Clarke and Wright algorithm only to initialize the vehicle-routing problem (VRP). The results demonstrate that the routing cost drops by 25% compared to the current solution. In (Cárdenas-Barrón et al., 2019 ), a heuristic method is introduced based on reducing and optimize approach (ROA) to address routing problems.  5.2.3 Meta-heuristic approaches Nowadays, meta-heuristic approaches have become the most popular in waste collection optimization. The meta-heuristic approaches can provide a sufficiently good solution to an optimization problem with limited computational capacity or incomplete information. Generally, the meta-heuristic methods are developed using the concepts of physical sciences or biological evolution. The meta-heuristic approaches are classified as follows: (1) population-based approaches, such as particle swarm optimization (PSO), ant colony optimization (ACO) and artificial bee colony (ABC) algorithm and the genetic algorithm (GA); and (2) trajectory-based approaches, such as the tabu search (TS) and simulated annealing (SA). The PSO algorithm is a popular meta-heuristic population-based optimization approach which uses the behaviors and movement of an organism such as bird flocking and fish schooling in searching for food. PSO has an easy implementation process and requires a few tuning parameters. Besides, PSO exhibits efficient memory capacity and low computational cost (Akhtar et al., 2016 ). A work in (Duman et al., 2019 ) applied PSO to develop a novel forecasting method focusing on the prediction of e-waste generation. The results illustrate that household income level and population density have a significant impact on the generation of e-waste. In (Hannan et al., 2018 ), a CVRP model is designed based on a modified particle swarm optimization (PSO) algorithm. The results indicate that the proposed model achieves the best waste collection and route optimization with regard to fuel consumption, travel length, cost and efficiency. The ACO is based on the behavior of an ant in a path that is designed using the deposition of pheromones by the previous ants. ACO is more advantageous than other methods in terms of convergence speed (Zhang and Xiong, 2018 ). In (Liu and He, 2012a ), a clustering-based improved multiple ACO is proposed to minimize the route distance of waste collection. The results indicate a decrease in route length by 31.1% and 31.9% with or without turns constraints, respectively. In (Nambiarl and Idicula, 2013 ), the static vehicle routing problem (SVRP) is transformed into a dynamic vehicle routing problem in SWC and accordingly, a modified ACO-based approach is applied to find the optimal routes to visit the waste collection facilitates. In (Ismail and Loh, 2009 ), ACO is employed to address waste collection problems aiming to reduce the traveling cost with the implementation of preventive restocking policy. In (Mancera-Galván et al., 2017 ), two ACO algorithms are suggested for waste collection optimization and the results show improvements with regard to route length minimization and routes re-design. The ABC is formulated using a natural foraging act of honey bees. The ABC is superior to other optimization methods concerning flexibility, easiness and robustness (Lin et al., 2018 ). In (Wei et al., 2019 ), the ABC optimization algorithm is employed to minimize the carbon emission cost of all vehicles during waste collection. The effectiveness of the proposed model is validated by experiments that demonstrate superior performance than other popular swarm-based optimization algorithms. The results indicate a reduction of 7.16% in carbon emissions by choosing an optimal waste disposal trip. The GA uses three operational stages including, reproduction, crossover, and mutation to upgrade the fitness function and accordingly achieve the final best solution. The GA has simple execution progression but has some weaknesses with respect to slow optimization response time (Dutta et al., 2020 ). GA has been widely used for waste optimization (Komly et al., 2012 ). In (Assaf and Saleh, 2017 ), a VRP for SWC is modeled through GA to find the optimal routes leading to the minimization of the traveled path and operational costs. The results indicate a reduction in travel distance by 66% while waste collected duration is limited to 2.3 h. The SA is a famous heuristic optimization technique, which is inspired by metallurgy annealing. In (Muttiah et al., 1996 ), The Markov-chain-based SA algorithm is suggested to search for optimal waste disposal location using GIS. In (Song et al., 2014 ), a forecast model based on SA is introduced to assess the multistep ahead prediction ability of waste generation in Washington, the United States. In (Babaee Tirkolaee et al., 2019 ), SA is proposed to address the VRP to minimize the total cost within permitted time windows. SA is also employed in (Asefi et al., 2017 ) to develop an integrated SWC network to address location-routing problem leading to a reduction in establishment cost and transportation cost. The TS is another meta-heuristic algorithm that enhances the performance of the local search (neighborhood search) by relaxing its basic rules. In (Shao et al., 2020 ), a waste collection synchronization mechanism is proposed using TS to find efficient vehicle routes and achieve maximum profitability. In (Gómez et al., 2015 ), TS is employed to collect the waste bins in rural areas using multi-objective adaptive memory programming (MOAMP). An appropriate objective function helps to achieve the target in SWC with respect to cost, route, time, energy, emissions etc. subject to satisfying all constraints such as demand, capacity, human force, waste facilities etc. Nevertheless, the formulation of multi-objective functions needs laborious efforts and may not be accurate due to various uncertainties and complexity in SWC. Numerous SWC modeling approaches have been proposed in literatures to solve SWC issues. The conventional approaches can deliver satisfactory results under small-scale models but suffer from predicting good results against various uncertainties. The heuristic methods can achieve near-optimal solutions; however, they lack in precision and requires a long implementation time. The meta-heuristic methods have the flexibility in choosing suitable objectives, constraints under different conditions; nevertheless, they have shortcomings in terms of parameter setting and computation complexity. The fuzzy logic and probabilistic methods are very effective in modeling the SWC system under various uncertainties. Nonetheless, they require realistic data and human experience, thus leading to a complex framework. Various optimization methods have been employed to achieve significant solutions in the SWC system. However, the integration of optimization algorithms in a sophisticated and complicated system like SWC is an open research issue to be explored. PSO and ABC are quick, robust, and have few control parameters. But they have negative points on search capability which can be trapped into local minima. ACO has complex theoretical analysis but has guaranteed convergence speed and good adaption to changes in new solutions. GA and SA have an easy implementation process but suffer from slow convergence speed. TS has short computation time but has limitations with regard to search exploration, low efficiency and slow convergence. In recent times, the usage of artificial intelligence and information technology in SWC have become increasingly popular due to their high capability in assessing the level of garbage in waste containers, the quantity of waste as well as detecting and classifying the waste bin level. Various Data acquisition, communication and identification technologies have employed in SWC including sensors, GSM, ZigBee, GPRS, GPS etc. Besides, GIS is utilized to collect and optimize waste through the determination of suitable landfill. Although GIS is efficient in delivering the shortest route and time, the performance depends on the decision-making process with supporting tools and planning. Although researchers and municipalities have made great efforts to develop a new and effective SWC system, several key issues need to be taken into account to operate SWC model efficiently under the practical environment. Some of the key challenges of SWC are summarized below. 6.1 Multi-objective issues The formulation of multi-objective function is a laborious task since it is difficult to optimize two self-contradictory objectives at the same time without making one worse. For instance, there is very little chance that both waste content and distance or time will be optimized at the same time. Nevertheless, it is possible to improve the value of one objective without deteriorating that of the other in multi-objective optimization by using the set of Pareto optimal solutions. To tackle the issues of multi-objective problems, all the objectives are converted either into a single objective or some of these may be converted into constraints. But these techniques are not very effective as they generate a single solution per calculation. To this point, Pareto optimal solutions cannot be guaranteed. To map all frontier sets, calculations should be repeated numerous times which decreases the computational performance.  6.2 Economic impact Solid waste collection cost depends on several factors such as the area of the municipality, geographic condition of the municipality (hilly, plain land, etc.), the population size of the area, the feature of the area (residential, industrial, commercial, etc.), transportation modes used in the collection, number of crews assigned in the task, etc. Actually, a large percentage of operational cost is spent on fuel consumption during waste collection that depends on the factors of driving distance of the road and its gradient, condition of the collection vehicle (Tavares et al., 2009 ), as well as quantity of the waste load (Larsen et al., 2009 ). Therefore, designing an effective solid waste collection system considering the economic condition of the municipality is a challenge and a lot of factors are needed to be considered.  6.3 Environmental impact The environmental impacts can be categorized mainly in three categories: noise control, traffic congestion and air pollution. Noise control is taken into account especially in busy institutional and commercial areas where it may have a disturbing effect on the surroundings. Whereas, traffic congestion is a problem that rises other problems such as long collection hours, more fuel consumption, more expense, air pollution, etc. Air pollution is the biggest concern during solid waste collection. The waste collection vehicles may emit a significant amount of pollutants including GHGs (Tavares et al., 2009 ). It is seen that by using compressed natural gas (CNG) and liquefied natural gas (LNG), tail-pipe emissions of collection vehicles are significantly less than the diesel-driven collection vehicle (Maimoun et al., 2016 ). However, the availability of these fuels is uncertain and a huge amount of cost is required to convert existing vehicles to use natural gas.  6.4 Available technologies With the development of technology, the use of wireless sensor networks (WSN) is also increasing in SWC to monitor the waste collection process. For instance, in European countries, RFID technology with different sensors are employed for waste collection and recyclables (Hannan et al., 2011 ). In Malaysia, the garbage truck monitoring system is introduced via GPS and GPRS (Hannan et al., 2015 ). Although the application of different technology for waste collection monitoring has already been started, till now it is mostly in high-income countries only. Therefore, the feasibility of introducing these technologies in middle and low-income countries is yet to be studied.  6.5 Type of waste The same solid waste collection process cannot be implemented for collecting all types of waste. The waste quantity and composition are different in residential and commercial areas. Residential areas usually produce a small amount of waste including organic waste, while the commercial area is dominated by demolition and construction waste in large numbers (Singh et al., 2011 ). Also in residential areas, waste collection is done by traveling the street back and forth, whereas, in commercial areas waste collection is more location focused (Wy et al., 2013 ). Therefore, making different collection strategies for the different waste generated area makes the waste collection system complicated.  6.6 Safety issues The safety of the crew assigned to waste collection is an important factor. As they always keep in touch with different waste components, deterioration of their health is quite apparent. They face more problems, specifically, in developing countries due to the lack of proper law enforcement. A portion of hazardous material produced in bulk in developed countries is handled properly in an expensive manner, whereas, in low-income countries, a large percentage of the generated waste is thrown in open spaces, resulting in an unhealthy condition.  6.7 Waste disposal issues The leakage of toxicants from waste dumps significantly pollutes local soil, water and groundwater. The aging of these landfills seriously affects the mineralization process and dynamics of toxic elements in wastes (Gupta et al., 2015 ). Hence, it is essential to study on aging and physical properties of waste. Paul et al. (2019)  explored the characteristics of waste concerning different layers related to aging. Also, the study estimated the environmental hazards and waste-to-wealth conversion efficiency through vermicomposting technology. Moreover, waste can be converted into manure which offers many benefits in terms of retaining nutrients, helping the growth of plants, promoting microbial activity and enhancing soil fertility (Sudharmaidevi et al., 2017 ). A study by (Yeheyis et al., 2013 ), analyzed the lifecycle of industrial construction projects to design a sustainable and comprehensive construction and demolition (C & D) waste management system toward maximizing the 3R (reduce, reuse and recycle) and minimizing the waste disposal.   7 Solid waste collection in sustainable development goal The 2030 sustainable development goals (SDGs), proposed in 2015 offers a common vision for prosperity and peace for the planet and people (Vinuesa et al., 2020 ). SWC and optimization can be directly or indirectly linked to 10 out of 17 SDGs. SWC affects several domains of SDG including environment, public health, poverty and resource value. The affected domains include clean water, good health, terrestrial and marine ecosystems, clean energy, sustainable cities, economic development, access to green jobs and sustainable natural resource utilization. Table 8   and Fig. 2   illustrate the 10 SDGs with their related goals and links to the optimization of SWC introduced in this study. Several relevant studies have been explored to validate the relationship between SWC and the target of SDGs. For instance, SWC can ensure environmentally sound disposal of waste (Singh, 2019 ), eliminate open dumping (Pujara et al., 2019 ) and protect water, soil from chemically toxic waste (Chandra Manna et al., 2018 ) that is linked to the target of SDG 6.3, 12.4 and 15.1 respectively. Besides, SWC can generate renewable energy from (organic) waste which is associated with the target of SDG 7.2. Moreover, SWC can control a large number of carbon emissions (Rajaeifar et al., 2017 ) and prevent waste (especially plastics) from entering the oceans (Luttenberger, 2020 ) that can be related to the target of SDG 13 and 14.1 respectively. Furthermore, SWC technology improves air quality in cities (Hossain et al., 2011 ) and promotes urbanization as well as slum up-gradation through efficient recycling and management of solid waste (Azevedo et al., 2019 ), thus linking to the target of SDG 11.6 and 11.1, respectively. The optimized SWC also has positive impacts on human health by preventing many diseases such as Hepatitis A, B, C, and HIV/AIDS and malaria (Zolnikov et al., 2018 ) which can be linked to the target of SDG 3.2, 3.3 and 3.9. SWC can act as a sustainable model to promote economic progress (Elagroudy et al., 2016 ) and create potential job opportunities (Pini et al., 2019 ) which can be associated with the target of SDG 8.3 and 1.4, respectively. Moreover, SWC can minimize waste through the implementation of 3Rs (Reduce, Reuse and Recycle) (Laustsen, 2007 ) as well as reduce food losses (Lemaire and Limbourg, 2019 ), which is related to the target of SDG 12.5 and 12.3 respectively. Overall, the analyzed results show that optimization and the latest innovation employed in SWC can achieve the SDGs efficiently related to good health, poverty reduction, sustainable cities, marine emissions minimization and economic growth which contribute toward achieving the 10 out of the 17 SDGs. Thus, contributions of this study can increase the effort toward achieving SDG-2030 by controlling and optimizing the SWC, therefore contributing to social benefits, economic growth and environmental protection.  8 Conclusions and recommendations The numerous research and development are carried out on SWC modeling structure, and technology to lower cost, route, time, labour and carbon emissions. An improved and effective optimization strategy can ensure the resilient, sustainable, and economic operation of SWC systems. However, the coordination of optimization techniques and the latest technology employed in SWC needs more investigations to achieve the desired goal under various realistic constraints. Moreover, the role of SWC in achieving SDGs needs to be assessed to determine the substantial impacts on human life, sanitation, poverty, climate, and employment. This review summarizes a vast study of waste collection approaches including different models, constraints, optimization topics, technologies that have been introduced in the literature. From the studies reviewed, it is observed that conventional methods are used in common at an early stage. Then, heuristics and meta-heuristic have replaced the conventional methods due to the increased complexity of the collection problem and computational time. It is also noticed that the use of GIS is very common in all SWC modeling approaches. Currently, with the development of advanced technologies, including RFID, GPS, GPRS, etc., along with smart bins and different sensors are given huge attention. These technologies are being used to monitor the whole SWC scenario from a remote server and have the decision-making ability to deliver the best optimal solutions under various constraints. The review also demonstrates the strong relationship of the 10 (out of 17) sustainable development objectives with the SWC. However, the development of an intelligent SWC system remains a vital issue to be explored due to the lack of effective policies introduced in different countries. In the context of the above discussions, this review provides some useful recommendations for future research direction, such as.i) The objective functions are formulated with respect to fulfilling the constraints. However, in most cases, the list of constraints reported in literature are very common. The exiting SWC models are developed considering a few types of constraints. Various factors could influence the SWC model including waste collection rate, waste coverage, waste collection efficiency, personnel capacity, institutional arrangement, management plan, policy, legal frameworks, financial resources, budget allocation, political interest, social networks and social learning. Therefore, the maximum numbers of constraints need to be considered for making the study more accurate.  ii) The successful operation of the optimization algorithm depends on how the constraints are operated regardless of the changing environment. However, in majority cases, the conditions of constraints are challenging to sustain in real world environment. Specially, unavoidable uncertainty is an important issue in environmental impact assessment predictions. Thus, the inclusion of various uncertainties into constraints needs further studies.  iii) Numerous optimization methods have been employed in SWC; however, they have drawbacks in terms of slow convergence, complex theory, poor search capability and tiresome parameters tuning. Thus, the development of efficient and robust optimization methods in SWC is required to obtain better results in terms of cost, travel distance and emissions.  iv) The performance of optimization algorithms can be further enhanced with the integration of advanced technologies. Nevertheless, there are concerns on the deployment of ICT in SWC such as accuracy, transmission range, data transfer rate and security. Consequently, a strategy on ICT and optimization algorithm development is highly desirable to avoid the ICT deployment concerns. Hence, further studies should be conducted on the integration of optimization methods into recent technologies.  v) The main aims of SWC models are to reduce cost, travel distance and emission. Usually, these targets are fulfilled simultaneously through the construction of multi-objective functions. Nevertheless, multi-objective optimization may create contradiction by making one worse. Since it is difficult to reach the optimum solutions for all the objective functions simultaneously, the concept of compromise solutions can be employed. Thus, further attention is needed to improve the performance of Pareto optimal solutions with regards to equity and efficiency. Pareto optimality highlights the best segment between the multi-objective functions.  vi) The SWC has strong influences on health, economic, environment, and energy conservation, that subsequently build a relationship with SDGs. Since the significance and complexity of SWC are more prominent than before, the relationship between SWC and SDGs achievement needs more exploration towards future sustainability in the domain of environment, poverty reduction, health and economic growth.    The information provided in this review can play a vital role in the improvement of waste collection and management towards an economic and sustainable development in future research applications.
